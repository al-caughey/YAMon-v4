Access Restrictions

A better way to define which devices can access the Internet when...

Proceed with caution because overly broad rules could inadvertently prevent all devices from accessing your router!

If this occurs, unplugging your USB drive and manually rebooting your router should get you back in.

At some point in the future, this will list your restrictions. Right now, I need people to help test this functionality.

Organize your devices into groups on the Settings tab *before* creating access restrictions.

By default, all new devices get added to the `Unknown` group and that group gets blocked whenever a restriction is in effect (so that users cannot bypass the access restrictions by spoofing their MAC address). If you have not moved your devices and/or some of the others into another group, you might not be pleased with the results!

Export Settings

All of the settings,etc. for this web app are retained in localStorage (meaning that they are private to this browser on this computer). This is my quick & dirty method (emphasis on the latter) for copying your settings to different browser or device.

Copy the text from the field below to a file on a USB stick or email it to yourself and then open the `Settings` tab on the other machine (or browser) and click `Import`.

Import Settings

Paste the settings you exported previously into the field below and then click `Import`.

NOTE - this will replace all similarly named variables within the localStorage for this browser on this machine. It cannot be undone! I strongly recommend that you first export the settings from this machine... just in case!

Import ISP Data

Paste the data from your ISP's usage table into the field below [ISP link] and then click `Process`. Otherwise, click `Close` to dismiss this dialog.
